About Oxford University

Oxford University, formally known as the University of Oxford, is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the world. It is located in Oxford, England, and has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. The university's exact founding date is uncertain, but teaching is believed to have started as early as 1096. Today, Oxford is a leading institution for academic excellence, research, and intellectual achievement.

The university is organized as a collegiate system, consisting of 39 colleges and six permanent private halls. Each college is an independent institution within the larger university, with its own buildings, teaching staff, and unique traditions. Students are members of both a college and the wider university, creating a close-knit community within the larger academic environment.
